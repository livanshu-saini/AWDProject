Try Local Food Of Ajmer: From Places To Products Here Is The Best Of 2022



Ajmer is popular for its forts, palaces, rich heritage, culture, and traditions. But apart from all these famous attractions, the one thing that makes the much talking about the city is its varying and distinguished flavors of street food items and options. When in Ajmer, you will never be out of the food options that you can try. The mouth-watering and unique flavors of the Rajasthani food will make you fall in love with them. Here are some of the most famous options of street food in Jaipur that shouldn’t be missed for an unmatched taste.

1. Pyaaz Kachori


Just like any other city of Rajasthan, Ajmer is also known for its kachoris. And the best place for this deep fried delicacy is Jodhpur Sweets. This sweet and savoury shop at Naya Bazaar is one of the most popular places for authentic Rajasthani Kachori. They serve it with spicy potato curry and sweet red sauce (saaut chutney). Bet you’ve got your mouth watering already!

Location:

Jodhpur Sweets


2. Kadhi Kachori


If you’re looking for a curry variant of Kachori, you should definitely try out the Kadhi Kachori at Khandelwal Chaat Bhandar which is in Kesar Ganj. The spicy kachori immersed in tangy Kadhi gives the most unique taste that you’ll remember for a very long time. You might want go here on an empty stomach because can’t have just one.

Location:

Khandelwal Chaat Bhandar


3. Pani Puri


Situated at Madar Gate, Jai Jhulelal is the most popular pani puri stall in Ajmer. What makes it so unique is the pani tangy-minty water that has a very peculiar sweet taste. The stall is often crowded during evening time so you might want to go before the peak hour to catch the freshest of pani puri. The three decades old place also offers the most delicious Aloo Tikkis and Bhaji Pao. These too are worth a try.

Location:

Jai Jhulelal


4. Mutton Korma


Ajmer is also a treat for all you carnivores. The Mutton Korma at Madina hotel is the simplest recipe but has the most intricate taste which is the perfect mix of spicy and tangy. Have it with the special Indian breads they have in their menu. The combo is perfect for a long due lunch.

Location:

Madina Hotel


5. Falooda


If you’ve visited Ajmer in peak summers, you’ve totally earned yourself a Falooda from Indian Mix Ice Cream House. They serve the delicious falooda kulfi in a wine glass instead of a traditional plate. The blend of sugary syrups with vermicelli and ice cream is an explosion of multi-fold flavours in your mouth.

Location:

Indian Mix Ice Cream House
